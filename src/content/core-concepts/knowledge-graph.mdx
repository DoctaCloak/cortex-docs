# Knowledge Graph

Your knowledge graph is the living structure that emerges from your notes and their connections. Every note is a node, every link is an edge.

## Graph Properties

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Nodes** | Total notes in your graph |
| **Edges** | Total links between notes |
| **Density** | Edges / possible edges — higher = more interconnected |
| **Clusters** | Groups of densely connected notes (auto-detected) |

## How the Graph Grows

```
Week 1:   ○  ○  ○  ○  ○        (isolated notes)

Week 4:   ○──○  ○──○──○        (some connections)
              ╲╱
               ○

Month 3:  ○──○──○──○──○        (network emerges)
          │╲ │╱ │╲ │ ╱│
          ○──○──○──○──○
          │  │╲ │  │╱ │
          ○──○──○──○──○
```

Early on, notes are isolated islands. As you add more and link them (manually or automatically), clusters form. Eventually, your graph develops **emergent structure** — themes and connections you didn't plan.

## Viewing Your Graph

The **Observatory** page provides an interactive visualization:

- **Force-directed layout** positions related notes closer together
- **Color** indicates note type (facts, insights, decisions, etc.)
- **Size** reflects importance score
- **Edge color** shows relation type

## Graph Operations

### Reflect

The `reflect` operation analyzes your graph for:

- **Clusters** — groups of 3+ notes that are densely interconnected
- **Bridge notes** — notes that connect otherwise separate clusters
- **Orphans** — notes with zero links that might need connecting
- **Synthesis opportunities** — clusters that could benefit from a summary note

### Decay and Prune

Over time, low-importance notes that haven't been accessed or updated lose relevance. The decay system:

1. Calculates a **staleness score** based on age and last access
2. Notes below the decay threshold are candidates for pruning
3. **Pinned notes** and notes with importance ≥ 0.7 are decay-resistant
4. Always run with `dry_run: true` first to preview what would be pruned
