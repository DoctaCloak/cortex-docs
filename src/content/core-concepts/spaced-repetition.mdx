# Spaced Repetition

Spaced repetition is a learning technique that reviews information at increasing intervals, timed to when you're about to forget it. Cortex automatically generates flashcards from your notes and schedules them using an adaptive algorithm.

## The Forgetting Curve

In 1885, Hermann Ebbinghaus discovered that memory decays exponentially over time. Without review, you forget:

- **50%** within 1 hour
- **70%** within 24 hours
- **90%** within 1 week

Spaced repetition fights this by reviewing at optimal intervals:

```
Day 1    Day 3    Day 7    Day 14    Day 30    Day 90
  ✓        ✓        ✓        ✓         ✓         ✓
  └────────┴────────┴────────┴─────────┴─────────┘
              Intervals grow exponentially
```

Each successful review doubles (approximately) the interval until the next review.

## How Cortex Generates Flashcards

When you create a note with importance ≥ 0.5, Cortex can generate flashcards:

1. **Extract key claims** from the note content
2. **Generate question-answer pairs** using the note's declarative title
3. **Schedule the first review** for the next day
4. **Adapt intervals** based on your responses

### Example

**Note**: "Active recall is more effective than passive review"

**Generated flashcard**:
- **Q**: What learning technique is more effective than passive review (re-reading, highlighting)?
- **A**: Active recall — testing yourself on the material, even before you feel ready.

## Review Flow

When a flashcard is due:

1. You see the **question**
2. You attempt to recall the answer
3. You reveal the answer and rate your recall:
   - **Again** — Forgot completely. Reset interval to 1 day.
   - **Hard** — Partially remembered. Interval × 1.2
   - **Good** — Remembered correctly. Interval × 2.0
   - **Easy** — Instant recall. Interval × 2.5

## Importance and Review Priority

Not all notes are equal. The importance score affects review scheduling:

- **High importance (0.8+)**: Reviewed more frequently, shorter initial intervals
- **Medium importance (0.5–0.7)**: Standard schedule
- **Low importance (< 0.5)**: No automatic flashcard generation

## Integration with Knowledge Graph

Flashcards aren't isolated — they connect back to your knowledge graph:

- Each flashcard links to its source note
- Reviewing a flashcard reinforces the note's position in your memory
- Notes that are frequently reviewed gain "memory strength" that boosts their recall ranking
- Related notes are sometimes shown as context during review
